<objective>
Fix HVAC energy calculation by removing the thermal mass correction factor, resolving annual cooling energy under-prediction (53-67% below reference).

Purpose: Verification identified that solar gains are being calculated (15.50 MWh annual) and integrated into thermal network (max temp in range), but HVAC energy tracking shows significantly lower cooling than expected (1.01 MWh vs 2.13-3.67 MWh reference). Investigation revealed a **double correction bug**: `thermal_mass_correction_factor` (0.20 for Case 900) is applied multiplicatively to hvac_output_raw, then Plan 03-02's energy subtraction approach also tries to subtract thermal mass energy change, causing **over-correction** (7.29 MWh or 11.20 MWh instead of 2.13-3.67 MWh target).

**Root Cause Analysis:**
The `thermal_mass_correction_factor` is legacy code for peak load tracking (empirical correction for high-mass vs low-mass cases). It's being used in TWO places:

1. **Energy calculation** (line 1948): `let hvac_output_energy = hvac_output_raw * self.thermal_mass_correction_factor;`
2. **Peak load tracking** (lines 1930-1943): Applied multiplicatively to peak values

The problem is that `hvac_output_raw` is calculated from the 5R1C thermal network physics using **Ti_free** (free-floating temperature), which **already includes thermal mass effects** through:
- `h_tr_em` and `h_tr_ms` conductances (thermal mass coupling)
- Thermal capacitance `Cm` (thermal mass response rate)
- Implicit/explicit Euler integration (Cm × ΔTm/dt)

Therefore, applying a separate multiplicative correction factor is **redundant and incorrect**. The thermal mass effects are PHYSICALLY BAKED INTO the HVAC demand calculation via the thermal network.

Output: Corrected HVAC energy calculation that uses hvac_output_raw directly (no multiplicative factor), resulting in Case 900 annual cooling energy within [2.13, 3.67] MWh reference.
</objective>
