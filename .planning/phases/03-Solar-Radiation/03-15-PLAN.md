   - Document that reference programs (EnergyPlus, ESP-r, TRNSYS) may use different thermal network structures (6R2C, 8R3C)
   - Note that annual energy over-prediction for Case 900 is expected behavior with current model

2. **Annual Energy Over-Prediction Root Cause**
   - High h_tr_em/h_tr_ms coupling ratio (0.0525) causes thermal mass to exchange 95% with interior, 5% with exterior
   - Thermal mass follows interior temperature instead of exterior temperature
   - During winter: Thermal mass releases heat to interior (high h_tr_ms = 1092 W/K), increasing heating demand
   - During cooling: Thermal mass absorbs solar heat but releases to interior (high h_tr_ms), reducing cooling benefit
   - Annual energy accumulates over 8760 hours, not just seasonal extremes

3. **Mode-Specific Coupling Improvement**
   - Document Plan 03-14 approach: separate heating/cooling coupling parameters
   - Heating mode: h_tr_em_heating = 0.15x base coupling (8.61 W/K)
   - Cooling mode: h_tr_em_cooling = 1.05x base coupling (60.29 W/K)
   - Result: 22% improvement in annual heating energy (5.35 MWh vs 6.87 MWh baseline)
   - Status: Most sophisticated approach attempted, but still above reference

4. **What Works Well**
   - Solar radiation integration complete (SOLAR-01 through SOLAR-04)
   - Peak loads within ASHRAE 140 reference ranges (heating 2.10 kW, cooling 3.56 kW)
   - Free-floating max temperature within reference range (41.62°C)
   - Temperature swing reduction improved from 9.9% to 13.7%
   - HVAC demand calculation validated as correct per ISO 13790

5. **Future Research Directions**
   - Investigate ASHRAE 140 reference implementation thermal network structure
   - Consider implementing 6R2C or 8R3C model for high-mass buildings

6. **Impact on Other Cases**
