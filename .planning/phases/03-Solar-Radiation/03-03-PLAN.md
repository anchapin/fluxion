    2. Identify issues with current peak load tracking:
       - **Issue 1: Steady-state heat loss approximation**
         - Current method: ss_heat_loss = h_ext * (setpoint - outdoor_temp)
         - This assumes building reaches steady-state instantly
         - For high-mass buildings, thermal mass buffers temperature swings, reducing peak loads
         - The thermal_mass_correction_factor (peak_correction) is applied, but may not be sufficient

       - **Issue 2: Peak correction factor**
         - peak_thermal_mass_correction_factor is applied to steady-state heat loss
         - This is a multiplicative correction that may not accurately capture thermal mass buffering
         - The correction factor may be too low, resulting in under-prediction of peak loads

       - **Issue 3: Timing of peak loads**
         - Peak loads should occur when outdoor temp is most extreme (coldest winter day, hottest summer day)
         - Thermal mass effects cause peak loads to be lower than steady-state calculation
         - Current method may over-correct or under-correct depending on the factor value

       - **Issue 4: Free-floating temperature not used for peak tracking**
         - Peak tracking uses outdoor temp difference: ss_heat_loss = h_ext * (setpoint - outdoor_temp)
         - Should use free-floating temp (t_i_free) which includes thermal mass buffering
         - Free-floating temp represents actual building response to outdoor conditions

    3. Propose fixes:
       - **Fix 1: Use free-floating temp for peak load calculation**
         - Replace outdoor_temp with t_i_free in ss_heat_loss calculation
         - This captures thermal mass buffering effect more accurately
         - hvac_demand = h_ext * (setpoint - t_i_free)

       - **Fix 2: Remove peak_thermal_mass_correction_factor**
         - If using free-floating temp, the correction factor may no longer be needed
         - Free-floating temp already includes thermal mass effects

       - **Fix 3: Track peak at HVAC demand point**
         - Peak load should be measured at hvac_power_demand output, not steady-state approximation
         - Use hvac_output_raw (actual HVAC demand) instead of ss_heat_loss
